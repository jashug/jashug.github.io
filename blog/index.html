Fun Wheels to Reinvent — a Blog-like-thing by Jasper Hugunin

Over the past 10 or so years, from elementary school through graduating college with bachelor's degrees in Math and Computer Science, I've worked on a lot of projects in my free time. Many of these projects have been doing what other people have done before, and doing it worse—reinventing the wheel.

Nevertheless, these projects were both fun and educational. I feel that they gave me a better understanding of both how things work and why they work that way. They gave me a chance to both practice making things and to explore what would happen if things were different.

Here, I'll try to collect a record of what I've done and why I enjoyed it. If you have some free time and are looking for a fun project, you might find something here that inspires you.

(Planned) Structure of a Post

In each post, I'll look at one of the projects I've done, focusing on four things:

  1. Context: Why did I want to do this project?
  2. Result: What did I end up creating?
  3. Skills: What skills did I practice in this project?
  4. Resources: What would someone else need to get started on the same project?

I plan to add content out of order, so if you want to know what I've recently written, your best bet might be the version history of this site.
